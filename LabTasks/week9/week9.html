Chromagram is used for analysing harmonic and melodic features of music. Chroma features roughly correspond to 12 pitch classes (C, C#, D, D#, E ,F, F#, G, G#, A, A#, B), which is why I have adapted the Python code to create 12 histograms - one for each pitch class. The differences in the histograms represent the differences in the three songs.

Task 2.2: Comparing the histograms

For this part of the task, I have decided to compare histograms computed from mel frequency cepstral coefficients for each track. MFCC is usually used to analyse timber, that is the tone colour or quality. Considering the three tracks I am comparing are all from different genres and do not have the same instruments present, the MFCCs should be different for all three tracks. This is indeed the case, as we can see from the histograms above. Moreover, the histograms for Gradual Sunrise and Mary Roose seem to be slighly more similar to each other, than either of them is to Lullaby. This makes sense since the former are an indie rock and a blues songs that have some similar instruments, while the latter is a classical song with only a piano (and a voice) present. However, all three sets of histograms are distinct from each other, with the biggest difference being feature 0.
